Latemar Controversy

Latemar is a carbonate platform with a radius of around 3 km located in North Italy. It was formed during Middle Triassic around westernmost area of Triassic Tethys under marine environment. The focus of this essay is on the top 470 m of the Latemar platform succession, which contains around 600 cycles, group of 5 thinning-upward megacycles.

The Latemar controversy is over the mechanism of Latemar formation. Intense debates exist among researchers on whether the strong cyclic character of Latemar was mainly autocyclic, meaning driven by external forcing, or allocyclic, driven by more local forcings. Whilst Goldhammer and Hinnov[1] proposed that the 600-700 cycles of Latemar were formed through 9-12 million years ago (mya), Brack and Kent[2] argued that the formation time was much shorter. As the Middle Triassic is about 10 million years, it is doubted that the formation of Latemar could take more than the whole middle Triassic.

One of the methods to date Latemar succession is to connect the layers in Latemar to somewhere else where the age is better dated and use that as the age of latemar formation. For example, if we can find some layers in Latemar corresponding some layers in buchenstein beds, then the period for latemar formation would be just the same as the time taken to form the corresponding buchenstein beds This brings us to the first method, biostratigraphy.

With biostratigraphy, fossils of ammonites and Daonella were sampled. (This is a pic of ammonites.) Dark ammonites: were found in basinal Buchenstein Beds, they were formed in pelagic environment. Brighter ammonites: found in Latemar, deposited in platform interior. But they are of the same type. so if same type of ammonites fossils can be found in both basinal bed and the Latemar, they are coeval Faunas L1–L4 are ammonoids and Daonella. As you probably all know, volcanic ashfalls contain zircons, which can be used isotope dating and deliver usmore precise, absolute ages compared to the biostratigraphic approach.

Method: - High-resolution, single-grain U-Pb age determinations have been performed on zircons from several individual volcaniclastic intercalations at Seceda and closely correlated sections at Bagolino and (later) Monte San Giorgio. - Newly-formed zircon deposits will contain no lead, meaning that any lead found in the mineral is radiogenic.  Important: - Which layers were picked (show a Pietra Verde layer above upper age threshold indicated by Seceda fossils coeval to Latemar fauna L4) Results: Ages of crystallization of the zircon grains are taken as an approximation for the time of deposition of the volcaniclastic layers. An age of 241.2 +0.8/-0.6 Ma (95% confidence level) has been obtained from zircon grains in the lower part of the Buchenstein Beds. These tuff beds correspond to the lowermost part of the cyclic succession at Latemar. An age of 238.0+0.4/-0.7 Ma obtained from zircons in a layer in the uppermost Buchenstein Beds corresponds to a level younger than the top of the cyclic succession at Latemar.

Normally, Middle Triassic includes 25 polarity intervals(which means around 0.5ma interval between magnetic polarity change), which suggests that we cannot be anywhere close to 9-12 Ma of deposition time, but that this 470 m of Latemar was formed in less than 1 ma.

We can see with dating method developing over time, we have shorter and shorter time constraint on Latemar formation period. Earlier it was dated on Bagolino, later the dating was taken directly on Latemar. And a latest paper in 2018 agreed the ~1ma formation time. In addition it mentioned a thin layer of less than 1 meter in that region showed precession feature. But the counter group insists on that this whole succession was formed with Milankovitch character.
